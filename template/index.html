About Me

I took a different path in life, and started my own cleaning business with my twin sister after high school. We ran the business together for three years and decided o take the skills we had learned and move on to other opportunities. I took position at Jimmy Johns and, in one year, worked my way up from cashier to . Although the company encouraged further growth, I found myself facing a choice; take on more responsibility (requiring me to move out of state) or stay in Nashville in my current position. As a native Nashvillian, I knew moving wasn't the right option for me, so I found myself looking for a new career. I wanted a career with room to grow, where the possibilities would be endless. My family kept telling me to try software development. Everything I learned about software made it the obvious choice. I joined the Jumpstart program at NSS, and fell in love. My family had been right all along! Shortly after finishing the Jumpstart program I applied for the 6 month course and was accepted. I am lucky to have the opportunity to continually learn and grow with this career. The challenges motivate me to expand my knowledge, feed my creativity, and become a better developer.

My Projects

Projects



  • The Problem

    As an aspiring thru hiker I have realized there aren't any good platforms to organize your drop boxes for a several month journey. Sure, we have spreadsheets but those can get easily disorganized and can be confusing for people to use. I want to make a single page app that will allow the user to easily see all of the their boxes and the contents in each one they have prepped.

    The Solution

    The app will allow users to add a box to their list of boxes and they can input contents and the date to ship them for each one. They will have the option to add what type of food, gear, first aid and apparel in each one. When the user logs they will be able to access all of their saved boxes in the boxes tab of the app.
